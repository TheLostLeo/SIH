History of the Indian Constitution

The Indian Constitution was adopted on November 26, 1949, and came into effect on January 26, 1950, replacing the Government of India Act of 1935 as the governing document of India. The Constitution was drafted by the Constituent Assembly, which was established in 1946 to frame a new constitution for independent India.


          Key Timelines
            
            1946: Britain decides to grant independence to India, and the Cabinet Mission is dispatched to India to discuss the modalities for transfer of power.
            August 14, 1947: Proposal for creation of committees is tabled.
            August 29, 1947: Drafting Committee is established.
            December 6, 1947: Constituent Assembly formally convenes for the first time, following elections, to start the process of writing a constitution.
            November 4, 1947: Draft is finalized and submitted.
            1948-1949: Constituent Assembly meets in sessions open to the public.
            November 26, 1949: Constituent Assembly adopts the final draft, making it official.
            January 26, 1950: Entry into force of the new constitution.
            Committees of the Constituent Assembly
            
            Drafting Committee: Responsible for drafting the actual text of the Constitution, chaired by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.
            Provincial Constitution Committee: Drafted provisions related to state governments, chaired by Vallabhbhai Patel.
            States Committee: Negotiated with princely states for their integration into the Indian Union, chaired by Jawaharlal Nehru.
            Philosophical Structure
            
            The Objective Resolution, given by Jawaharlal Nehru on December 13, 1946, laid the philosophical structure of the Indian Constitution, which was passed on July 22, 1947.

            Characteristics
            
            The Indian Constitution is a codified and enacted document, adopted by a single body, the Constituent Assembly.
            It is a Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic with a parliamentary system of government.
            The Constitution has undergone several amendments since its adoption, with the most recent being the 101st Amendment in 2018.
