Preamble

WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC and to secure to all its citizens: JUSTICE, social, economic and political, LIBERTY of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship, EQUALITY of status and of opportunity, and to promote among them all FRATERNITY assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation
IN OUR CONSTITUENT ASSEMBLY this twenty-sixth day of November, 1949, do HEREBY ADOPT, ENACT AND GIVE TO OURSELVES THIS CONSTITUTION

Fundamental right

The Indian Constitution guarantees six fundamental rights to its citizens, which are listed in Articles 12â€“35:

  • Right to equality
  • Right to freedom
  • Right against exploitation
  • Right to freedom of religion
  • Cultural and educational rights
  • Right to constitutional remedies
Some of these rights include:
  • Right against exploitation: Prohibits forced labor, child labor, and human trafficking
  • Right to freedom of religion: Allows citizens to practice, propagate, and profess their religion
  • Cultural and educational rights: Allows citizens to conserve their culture, language, and script
  • Right to constitutional remedies: Allows citizens to enforce their fundamental rights through the courts
The courts are required to examine every law in light of the fundamental rights and can invalidate any law that goes against them. The Constitution also includes 11 fundamental duties for citizens in Article 51(A)

Fundamental duties

The Fundamental Duties of the Indian Constitution are a set of duties that every citizen of India is expected to follow:

  • Safeguard public property: Citizens are expected to protect public property.
  • Defend the country: Citizens are expected to defend the country and render national service if called upon to do so.
  • Protect the unity of India: Citizens are expected to uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India.
  • Respect the Constitution: Citizens are expected to respect and uphold the Constitution, its ideals, and institutions, the National Flag, and the National Anthem.
  • ideals of the freedom struggle: Citizens are expected to cherish and follow the noble ideals that inspired India's national struggle for freedom.
  • Develop scientific temper: Citizens are expected to develop a scientific temper.
The Fundamental Duties are significant because they: Promote civic consciousness, Promote people's participation, Inculcate moral and ethical values, Promote democratic principles, and Promote social welfare.
